Audacious Goals for an Audacious Startup

Creating a successful hard-tech startup demands more than just an innovative idea, it requires articulating a bold vision that inspires both your team and potential investors. At Commonwealth Fusion Systems (CFS), I recognized early on that clearly defining our audacious goals would crystalize our internal strategy and make our path to creating a new industry tangible and compelling.

My approach was influenced by Jim Collins' concept of the Big, Hairy, Audacious Goal (BHAG), a strategy designed to drive transformational growth:

The best BHAGs require both building for the long term AND exuding a relentless sense of urgency: What do we need to do today, with monomaniacal focus, and tomorrow, and the next day, to defy the probabilities and ultimately achieve our BHAG?
Collins illustrates this through examples such as Microsoft's early mission of "a computer on every desk and in every home," and SpaceX’s bold ambition of "making humans multiplanetary."

While Collins’ BHAG framework provided an inspiration, at CFS I adapted it to directly reflect the specific milestones needed to transform our core innovation, high-field superconducting magnets, into an economically viable fusion energy industry. The primary change was adding a series of increasingly audacious goals that built up to the BHAG that CFS is driving to.

Crystalizing the Vision

CFS began with a clear mission: to become the world’s leading fusion energy company. However, the concrete steps toward realizing that vision needed articulation. We intuitively knew the path: leveraging groundbreaking superconductors to develop high-field magnets, enabling compact, economic fusion power plants. Defining a structured, milestone-driven vision provided the necessary clarity both internally and externally.

The Four Audacious Goals Defined

1. Revolutionize high field magnets in 3 years, enabling a new class of high field fusion

Why Audacious? Traditional research timelines, guided by governmental and academic experts, projected a 5-10 year development period for the magnets CFS and MIT developed. Our plan compressed this dramatically, setting a 3-year goal. This aggressive timeline was crucial in answering investors' "why now?", as new magnet technologies would fundamentally shift the reality of fusion, offering a faster, more economical route.

Impact: It clearly answered investor question on "why now?", explicitly outlining what their investments would achieve, thus providing compelling urgency and clarity to secure funding. This goal forced the company to focus on the core enabling innovation and not be distracted by other, interesting but less fruitful opportunities.

2. Demonstrate net fusion energy by 2025, making fusion real for the first time (Q>1) in SPARC

Why Audacious? Achieving net fusion energy has historically eluded fusion researchers. Prior machines like JET approached but did not surpass the crucial threshold with sustainable, scalable technology. The National Ignition Facility has since surpassed Q>1, but with base technologies that are far from commercialization. SPARC’s design, targeting Q=11, and technology is a leap from experimental demonstration to scalable commercial viability, significantly outperforming existing machines and validating the practical potential of fusion.

Impact: It signals investors and stakeholders when fusion technology will genuinely arrive at commercial potential, marking the beginning of a new industrial sector. This goal forces the company to focus on growing and executing a complex technical project at scale, raising ~10x the money ($200M → $1.8B) and growing the team ~10x in the process (100 → 1,000 employees).

3. Produce economic fusion power by 2033, Q>10 on a path to targets of <$4/W and <6¢/kWh

Why Audacious? Transitioning from a successful demonstration (SPARC) to a commercially viable fusion power plant (ARC) represents an enormous technical, operational, and market-facing leap. While the first ARC may not be economically competitive, it will be the first in a series of ARCs that must eventually demonstrate continuous electricity production at competitive prices.

Impact: This goal forces the organization to mature rapidly from experimental operations to production-scale industrialization, meeting stringent cost, efficiency, and reliability standards demanded by customers.

4. Displace fossil fuel production of energy by 2050, creating a clean future for all

Why Audacious? Scaling to a global fusion energy industry, producing hundreds of plants annually, represents the ultimate transformation. It goes beyond technical challenges, encompassing global manufacturing, supply chain management, and long-term economic competitiveness.

Impact: This goal is the rallying cry for the company, underscoring the societal and environmental impact CFS aims to achieve. It aligns investor motivations and employee commitment, creating a lasting, transformative legacy.

Evolution Through Feedback

Initially, CFS’ vision was ambitious but lacked specificity in communicating the technological milestones and scale. Our first attempt in 2017 had broader statements without as clear, incremental impacts. Critical feedback from investors and advisors guided us toward the more structured, measurable, and compelling set of goals detailed above. Each iteration clarified our strategic milestones and deepened our ability to clearly communicate our transformational journey.

What Makes These Goals Powerful?

A list of powerful, audacious goals clearly and vividly:

  • Defines a tangible vision with measurable outcomes.
  • Provides logical continuity, each milestone reinforcing the next.
  • Answers critical investor questions of “why now?” and “what impact does my investment have?”
  • Motivates internal teams by presenting ambitious yet achievable milestones.
  • Focuses the company on what it has to do at each stage and what it has to grow into to do it.

To effectively implement these criteria, leaders should engage their teams in strategic discussions and align goal-setting with organizational values. This ensures that the goals are relevant, actionable, and inspiring, driving organizational growth and directing innovation.

Lessons for Founders

  • Begin with Clarity: Clearly articulate your key technology breakthrough and its direct impact on your industry.
  • Sequential Goals: Structure your goals so each logically leads to the next, demonstrating progressive achievement and scalability.
  • Bold, Yet Credible: Set aggressive timelines that motivate innovation but remain achievable.
  • Investor-Centric: Explicitly address why your technology matters now, answering investors' core questions about timing, differentiation, and impact.
  • Iterative Improvement: Your first draft won’t be perfect. Test your vision with external stakeholders and incorporate their feedback to refine and sharpen your messaging.

Conclusions

Your startup’s vision must not only be bold and audacious but precisely articulated, structured around clear, measurable milestones that directly resonate with investors and inspire your team. The evolution and crystallization of your goals are a continuous process, but clearly defined audacious goals provide the necessary roadmap for both internal focus and external excitement.
